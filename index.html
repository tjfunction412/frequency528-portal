Phase 2.5 — Non-Search Lead Vectors

Priority
HIGH (parallel with 1 & 2)
Cost
$0 (time only)
Effort
Ongoing
Owner
TJ + Ryan
Core Insight: High-value B2B buyers (festival producers, corporate planners) are NOT primarily finding vendors through Google. They source through peer recommendations, production communities, industry directories, and vendor networks.

Six Lead Vectors

1. Festival Prospect Database

Every festival that ran silent disco in 2024–2025 is a pre-qualified prospect. Research and build a list of 50+ festivals in drive radius. Feed into CRM.

2. Event Planner Communities

Join Facebook groups, LinkedIn, Reddit. Don't spam — contribute genuinely. When someone asks for silent disco vendors, be there.

3. Industry Directories

List on GigSalad, The Bash, Thumbtack, WeddingWire. Free or low-cost. High event planner traffic.

4. Festival Vendor Applications

Most mid-size festivals (5K–50K attendance) have vendor/entertainment submission processes. Direct B2B sales channel most operators ignore.

5. Venue Relationships

Target rooftops, amphitheaters, corporate venues, wedding spaces, breweries. One venue coordinator = multiple referrals/year.

6. Network Reactivation

Audit past clients and contacts from last 5 years. Send personal reactivation message (not a blast). Ask for referrals explicitly.

30-Day Priorities

  • Start festival prospect list (50+ in drive radius)
  • Set up GigSalad + The Bash listings
  • Join 3–5 relevant Facebook groups
  • Ryan updates LinkedIn (rental focus)
  • ID top 10 venue targets in Pittsburgh metro
  • Send reactivation outreach to 20 warmest past contacts

Phase 3 — CRM, Forms & Lead Pipeline Setup

Priority
HIGH
Cost
$6/mo (Gmail)
Time
1 weekend setup
Owner
TJ (build) + Ryan (operate)
The Problem: Every lead lands in email and either gets handled or gets buried. No pipeline visibility. Leads are disappearing.

The Stack

Tool Purpose Cost
Tally.so Inquiry form on website Free
HubSpot Free CRM, pipeline, contact tracking Free
Gmail / Google Workspace Email communication $6/mo
Zapier (free tier) Tally → HubSpot automation Free

Pipeline Stages

  • New Inquiry: Form submitted, not yet reviewed
  • Qualified: Date, location, headcount confirmed viable
  • Quote Sent: Pricing sent to prospect
  • Follow Up: Quote sent, awaiting response
  • Booked: Deposit received, date confirmed
  • Completed: Event done, review request sent
  • Lost: No response after 3 follow-ups

Inquiry Form Fields

  • Name / Organization
  • Email, Phone
  • Event Date
  • Event Location (City, State)
  • Event Type (dropdown: Festival, Corporate, Wedding, Birthday, Other)
  • Expected Guest Count
  • How did you hear about us? (dropdown: Google, Maps, Instagram, Facebook, Referral, GigSalad, Other)
  • Additional Notes

Automation: Tally → HubSpot

Every form submission automatically creates a contact + deal in HubSpot. Ryan gets notified. Zero manual entry.

Follow-Up Sequences

Quote Sent → No Response:

  • Day 3: Check-in
  • Day 7: Gentle reminder
  • Day 14: Final follow-up
  • Day 21: Move to Lost

Completed → Review Request:

  • Day 1 post-event: Thank you + Google review link
  • Day 7: One follow-up if no review yet

Success Metrics (60 days post-setup)

  • Zero leads lost to inbox burial
  • Every inquiry has a HubSpot deal within 24 hours
  • Quote response time under 48 hours
  • Follow-up sequences running on every open deal
  • Monthly pipeline review happening

Priority

Cost

Time
1 week setup

Guiding Principle: Paid search amplifies a working funnel. It doesn't fix a broken one. Don't spend a dollar until Phases 1, 2, and 3 are live.

Market Reality Check

From Google Trends research:

  • "Silent disco rental" — anchor term, consistent demand, low competition ✓
  • "Silent disco near me" — consumer intent (event-goer), not Ryan's buyer ✗
  • "Festival silent disco" — meaningful seasonal spikes (April–Aug) ✓
  • "Headphone party rental" — dead term, skip it ✗

This is a favorable paid search environment. Low competition + niche category = low cost-per-click and strong ROI potential.

Campaign Structure (Google Search)

  • Campaign 1 — Core Rental (Always On): "silent disco rental", "silent disco Pittsburgh", "silent disco Pennsylvania", regional variants
  • Campaign 2 — Festival Season (April–Aug): "festival silent disco", "silent disco festival rental", "festival entertainment rental"
  • Campaign 3 — Corporate Events (Always On): "corporate silent disco", "team building silent disco", "corporate entertainment rental"

Budget Recommendations

  • Starting: $200–$300/mo (enough to get real data)
  • Scaling: $400–$600/mo (once conversion data shows CPL under $30)
  • Seasonal: Increase April–August (festival season)

Budget Allocation

  • Core Rental: 50% (highest intent, always on)
  • Festival Season: 30% (April–August only)
  • Corporate & Events: 20% (build over time)

Tracking Setup (Non-Negotiable)

  • Google Analytics 4 installed
  • Google Ads conversion tracking
  • Form submission tracked as conversion event
  • Phone call tracking enabled
  • UTM parameters on all ad URLs
  • HubSpot lead source field capturing "Google Ads"

Success Metrics (90 days post-launch)

  • Cost per lead under $30
  • Click-through rate above 5% on core keywords
  • Conversion rate (click to inquiry) above 8%
  • At least 2–3 booked events from paid search
  • Quality Score 7+ on primary keywords
  • ROAS positive by month 3

Key Market Intelligence

Finding Implication
"Silent disco rental" = anchor term, consistent 5-year search demand Core keyword for search + ads. Good organic volume + low competition.
"Silent disco near me" = consumer event-goer intent, NOT B2B buyer Don't bid on this in ads. This is people looking to attend events, not planners.
"Silent disco rental near me" = near-zero search volume B2B buyers don't append "near me" — Google knows their location already.
High-value B2B buyers source through communities, not Google search Non-search channels (Phase 2.5) are equally critical as search infrastructure.
PA, OH, WV, MD, NY = underserved in search data Ryan's natural drive radius has weak competitor presence. Geographic advantage.
Festival season (April–August) has meaningful search spikes Ramp up visibility and ad spend before April. Planning window is now.
